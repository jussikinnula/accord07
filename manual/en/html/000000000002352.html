DTC Troubleshooting: P0502 (17-8)
DTC P0502:
 VSS Circuit Malfunction
  1. Test-drive the vehicle with the speed more than 20 km/h (12.5 mph).
  1. Check the vehicle speed with the HDS.
Is the correct speed indicated?
YES -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VSS and the ECM.■
NO -
Go to Step 3 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the VSS 3P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and wait 30 seconds.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Connect VSS 3P connector terminal No. 1 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 30 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 29 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the VSS and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 30, then go to Step 24 .
  1. Measure voltage between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 17 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and wait 30 seconds.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Check for continuity between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the VSS and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 84, then go to Step 24 .
NO -
Go to Step 15 .
  1. Connect VSS 3P connector terminal No. 2 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 84 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 29 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the VSS and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 84, then go to Step 24 .
  1. Measure voltage between VSS 3P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 3.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 22 .
NO -
Go to Step 18 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and wait 30 seconds.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Connect VSS 3P connector terminal No. 3 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 54 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 29 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the VSS and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 54, then go to Step 24 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Replace the VSS.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive under these conditions:
    • Transmission in 3rd gear
    • Engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) or more
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P0502 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VSS and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete.■
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Test-drive under these conditions:
    • Transmission in 3rd gear
    • Engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) or more
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P0502 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VSS and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. ■
