A/C Magnet Valve Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE: Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if the fans are also inoperative, or if the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  1. Check the No. 30 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck.■
  1. Disconnect the magnet valve 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 2 terminal of the magnet valve 2P connector and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 30 fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the magnet valve.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reconnect the magnet valve 2P connector.
  1. Connect the No. 1 terminals of the magnet valve 2P connector to body ground with a jumper wire with the magnet valve connector connected.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Does the magnet valve operate?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Replace the magnet valve, and recheck.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect the magnet valve 2P connector.
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector B (16P).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 8 terminal of climate control unit connector B (16P) and the No. 1 terminal of the magnet valve 2P connector.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Check for loose wires or poor connections at climate control unit connector B (16P) and at the magnet valve 2P connector. If the connections are good, substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the climate control unit and the magnet valve.■
