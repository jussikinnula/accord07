Radiator Fan High Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE: Before beginning this troubleshooting procedure, refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  1. Check the No. 19 (30 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse and recheck.■
  1. Remove the radiator fan relay from the multi-fuse/relay box, and test it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the radiator fan relay .■
  1. Measure the voltage between radiator fan relay 4P socket terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the under-hood fuse/relay box and radiator fan relay 4P socket terminal No. 1.
  1. Connect radiator fan relay 4P socket terminal No. 1 and No. 2 with a jumper wire.
Does the radiator fan run?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Disconnect the jumper, and turn the ignition switch ON (II). Check for voltage between radiator fan relay 4P socket terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the under-hood fuse/relay box and radiator fan relay 4P socket terminal No. 4.■
  1. Disconnect the radiator fan motor 2P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between radiator fan relay 4P socket terminal No. 2 and radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Repair an open in the wire between the radiator fan relay 4P socket terminal No. 2 and radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 2.■
  1. Check for continuity between radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the radiator fan motor. ■
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for a poor ground at G301.■
  1. Reinstall the radiator fan relay.
  1. Disconnect the radiator fan switch (High) 2P connector.
  1. Connect radiator fan switch (High) 2P connector terminal No. 1 and No. 2 with a jumper wire.
Does the radiator fan run?
YES -
Replace the radiator fan switch (High). ■
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Remove the jumper wire, and measure the voltage between radiator fan switch (High) 2P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Check for an open in the wire between radiator fan switch (High) 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for a poor ground at G302.■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between radiator fan switch (High) 2P connector terminal No. 2 and the under-hood fuse/relay box.■
