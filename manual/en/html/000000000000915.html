A/T Clutch Pressure Control Solenoid Valve B and C Test
  1. Connect the HDS to the DLC (A).
  1. Choose Clutch Pressure Control (Linear) Solenoid B in Miscellaneous Test Menu on the HDS.
  1. Test the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valve B with the HDS.
  1. Follow instructions indicated on the HDS by the test result. If the HDS has not determined the cause of the failure, remove A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valve B and C test.
  1. Disconnect the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C connectors.
  1. Measure A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valve B resistance between the solenoid valve B terminals No. 1 and No. 2, and measure A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valve C resistance between the solenoid valve C terminals No. 1 and No. 2.
    Standard:
    3−10 Ω

  1. If the resistance of either A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valve is out of standard, replace the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C.
  1. Connect the battery positive terminal to the No. 1 terminal of the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C connectors, and connect the battery negative terminal to the No. 2 terminal. A clicking sound should be heard.
  1. If no sound is heard, remove the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C.
  1. Check the fluid passage of the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valve for dust or dirt.
  1. Connect the No. 1 terminal of the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C connectors to the battery positive terminal, and connect the No. 2 terminal to the battery negative terminal. Make sure the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C move.
  1. Disconnect one of the battery terminals, and check valve movement.
    NOTE: You can see valve movement through the fluid passage in the mounting surface of the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C body.
  1. If either valve binds or moves sluggishly, or if the solenoid valve does not operate, replace the A/T clutch pressure control solenoid valves B and C.
