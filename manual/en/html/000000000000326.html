DTC Troubleshooting: 4
DTC 4:
 An Open or Short in the Mode Control Motor Circuit
  1. Disconnect the mode control motor 7P connector.
  1. Disconnect the audio-HVAC module 22P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and the audio-HVAC module 22P connector terminals No. 2, 3, 4, 5, 9, 11, and 12 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair any short to body ground in the wire(s) between the audio-HVAC module and the mode control motor.■
NO -
Go to Step 4 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and check the same terminals for voltage.
Is there any voltage?
YES -
Repair any short to power in the wire(s) between the audio-HVAC module and the mode control motor. This short may also damage the printed circuit board. Repair the short to power before replacing the printed circuit board.■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and check for continuity between the following terminals of the audio-HVAC module 22P connector and the mode control motor 7P connector.

Is there continuity?
YES -
Check for loose wires or poor connections at the audio-HVAC module 22P connector and at the mode control motor 7P connector. If the connections are good, test the mode control motor. If the mode control motor is OK, substitute a known-good audio-HVAC module, and recheck.If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original printed circuit board.■

Repair any open in the wire(s) between the audio-HVAC module and the mode control motor.■
