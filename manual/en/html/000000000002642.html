Navigation System Symptom Troubleshooting - Audio driving instructions cannot be heard
Audio driving instructions cannot be heard
NOTE: Always check the connectors for poor connections or loose terminals.
  1. Push the display unit MENU key, and select ‘‘setting''.
  1. Check the audio driving instructions volume setting.
Is it set to OFF?
YES -
Set the volume to an audible level.■
NO -
Go to Step 3 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the navigation unit connector A (20P) and the audio unit 20P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and navigation unit connector A (20P) terminals No. 14, No. 15 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
If terminal No. 14 or No. 15 has continuity to ground, replace the affected shielded harness.■
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Check for continuity between navigation unit connector A (20P) terminal No. 14 and the audio unit 20P connector terminal No. 13 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
There is an open in the circuit between the navigation unit and audio unit. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the audio and navigation units. If a poor connection or loose terminal is found, replace the affected shielded harness.■
  1. Check for continuity between navigation unit connector A (20P) terminal No. 15 and the audio unit 20P connector terminal No. 12.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
There is an open in the circuit between the navigation unit and the audio unit. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the audio and navigation units. If a poor connection or loose terminal is found, replace the affected shield harness.■
  1. Substitute a known-good audio unit, and recheck.
Is there system OK?
YES -
Replace the audio unit. ■
NO -
Replace the navigation unit . ■
