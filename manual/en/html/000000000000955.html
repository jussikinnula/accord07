DTC Troubleshooting: P0712 (28-3)
DTC P0712:
 Short in ATF Temperature Sensor Circuit
NOTE:
  • Record all freeze data and review General Troubleshooting Information before you troubleshoot.
  • This code is caused by an electrical circuit problem and cannot be caused by a mechanical problem in the transmission.
  1. Check ATF temperature sensor voltage with the HDS in the A/T data list.
Is ATF temperature sensor voltage 0.07 V or less?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for intermittent short in the wires between the ATF temperature sensor and PCM.■
  1. Disconnect the shift solenoid harness connector at the shift solenoid valve cover.
  1. Check ATF temperature sensor voltage with the HDS.
Is ATF temperature sensor voltage 0.07 V or less?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Check for a short to ground in the shift solenoid harness wire in the transmission. If the wire is OK, replace ATF temperature sensor, then go to Step 8 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
  1. Disconnect PCM connector C (22P).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 6 terminal of the shift solenoid harness connector and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to ground in the wire between PCM connector terminal C14 and the shift solenoid harness connector, then go to Step 8 .
NO -
Check for a short to ground in the shift solenoid harness wire in the transmission. If the wire is OK, update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good PCM, replace the original PCM .■
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle for several minutes in the D position in all five gears.
  1. Monitor the OBD status for P0712 in the DTCs/Freeze Data in A/T Mode Menu for a pass/fail.
Does the result indicate a pass?
YES -
The problem has been corrected.■
NO -
Return to Step 1 and recheck.■
