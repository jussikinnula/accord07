Engine Oil Pan Removal
NOTE: Make sure to inspect the oil pump chain, when removing the oil pan.
  1. If the engine is out of the vehicle, go to Step 12 .
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the A/C compressor without disconnecting A/C line.
  1. Remove the lower torque rod (A), then remove the torque rod bracket (B).
  1. Remove the both subframe mid mounts mounting bolts.
  1. Remove the steering joint cover.
  1. Remove the steering gearbox from the subframe.
  1. Remove the subframe stiffener mounting bolts (A).
  1. Loosen the two subframe mounting bolts (B) so they are about 30 mm (1.2 in.) from the subframe.
  1. Remove the dipstick/tube (A) and O-ring (B).
  1. Remove the A/C compressor bracket.
  1. Remove the transmission mounting bolts.
  1. Remove the bolts securing the oil pan.
  1. Using a flat blade screwdriver, separate the oil pan from the block in the places shown.
  1. Inspect the oil pump chain.
