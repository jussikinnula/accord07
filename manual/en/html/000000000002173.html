Climate Control Power and Ground Circuit Troubleshooting
  1. Check the No. 30 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck.■
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector B (16P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 4 terminal of climate control unit connector B (16P) and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 30 fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the climate control unit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 6 and No. 15 terminals of climate control unit connector B (16P) and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Check for loose wires or poor connections at climate control unit connector B (16P). If the connections are good, substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the climate control unit and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G506.■
