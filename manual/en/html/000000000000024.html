Brake Hose Replacement
NOTE:
  • Before reassembling, check that all parts are free of dirt and other foreign particles.
  • Replace parts with new ones whenever specified to do so.
  • Do not spill brake fluid on the vehicle; it may damage the paint; if brake fluid gets on the paint, wash it off immediately with water.
  • To prevent dripping, cover the disconnected line joints with rags or shop towels.
  1. Replace the brake hose (A) if the hose is twisted, cracked, or if it leaks.
  1. Disconnect the brake hose from the brake line (B) using a 10 mm flare-nut wrench (C).
  1. Remove and discard the brake hose clip (A) from the brake hose (B).
  1. Remove the banjo bolt (C), and disconnect the brake hose from the caliper.
  1. Remove the brake hose from the knuckle.
  1. Install the brake hose (A) on the knuckle with flange bolts (B) first, then connect the brake hose to the caliper with the banjo bolt (C) and new sealing washers (D).
  1. Install the brake hose (A) on the upper brake hose bracket (B) with a new brake hose clip (C).
  1. Connect the brake line (D) to the brake hose.
  1. After installing the brake hose, bleed the brake system.
  1. Do the following checks:
    • Check the brake hose and line joint for leaks, and tighten if necessary.
    • Check the brake hoses for interference and twisting.
