LKAS Camera Adjustment (Aiming)
NOTE: When the LKAS camera is replaced or removed and attached, be sure to perform re-aiming of the LKAS camera. When the aiming is not completed, an adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator will appear and the DTC will be stored.
There are Static Aiming and Dynamic Aiming procedures, and they need to be done in that order.
Cautions for the Working Environment
  • Use the radar aimer set.
  • Use a copy of the designated target pattern.
  • Perform the aiming in a proper place where you can obtain the specified distance, between a target pattern and the vehicle. There are four target patterns (1,500 mm, 2,000 mm, 2,500 mm and 3,000 mm) for the different scale of inspection lines.
  • Make sure that there are no bright objects (sunlight, window, lights, or reflectors, etc.) or any objects which have a similar pattern.
  • Perform the aiming in a well-lighted area.
Cautions for the Vehicle
  • All wheels of the vehicle should touch the ground.
  • Make sure that the suspensions are not modified.
  • Check the air pressure of all the tyres. If the pressures are uneven, inflate the tyres to the specified value.
  • Do not place the wheels of the vehicle on a steel plate or a roller when performing the aiming.
  • The vehicle should be empty and not be loaded when performing the aiming.
NOTE: Empty vehicle: A full fuel tank, tools and a spare tyre are present. Unload any baggage other than and the spare tyre.
  • Make sure that all doors are closed when performing the aiming.
  • Do not put any objects on the instrument panel.
  • Make sure that there are no stains on the lens of the LKAS camera, and the interior and exterior surface of the front glass of the lens parts.
  • Set the steering wheel in the straight ahead position.
  • Do not operate the steering wheel after the vehicle is set in the aiming position.
  • Shift the lever to the P position and apply the parking brake.
  • If you are towing a trailer, unhitch the trailer.
Preparation
Prepare the following:
  • Radar aimer set 07AJ-SDA0100
  • HDS
  • Measuring tape and scale
  • Target pattern (original dimension)
  • Gummed tape, ballpoint pen, string (approx. 10 m), strings with weight
Precaution for the Static Camera Aiming
To complete the aiming, it is necessary to succeed all of three targets' (center, passenger's side, driver's side) detections. If the detection for any one of targets failed or aborted on the way, the condition will be the aiming uncompleted.
When the static camera aiming was failure for some reason, aiming failure code is displayed on the HDS. In this case, troubleshoot the indicated failure code with the HDS.

Implementation of the Static Camera Aiming
  1. Connect the HDS to the data link connector (DLC).
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Select the LKAS camera aiming from the HDS. At this time, the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator comes on and ‘‘CAMERA'' is displayed at the multi-information display (MID).
  1. Set the LKAS camera height to the HDS.
    NOTE: Choose the ‘‘ LKAS camera height setting value number'' which was recorded in Step 22 of the preparation for the aiming.
  1. Position the central point of the target pattern aligning with the central target position (F) on the floor.
    NOTE: For precise positioning, dangle the thread with the weight through the central point of the target pattern, and move the radar aimer set so that the front edge of the weight aligns with the mark. After positioning, remove the thread and the weight from the target pattern surface.
  1. Command ‘‘the central target detection'' from the HDS and the ‘‘CAMERA'' on the multi-information display begins to blink.
    NOTE: ‘‘CAMERA'' on the multi-information display continues to blink until the aiming is completed and goes off when the aiming is completed.
  1. Check that ‘‘the central target detection'' is succeeded on the HDS.
  1. Position the central point of the target pattern aligning with the target position (H) of the passenger's side on the floor.
  1. Command ‘‘the passenger's side target detection'' from the HDS.
  1. Check that ‘‘the passenger's side target detection'' is succeeded on the HDS.
  1. Position the central point of the target pattern aligning with the target position (G) of the driver's side on the floor.
  1. Command ‘‘the driver's side target detection'' from the HDS.
  1. Check that ‘‘the driver's side target detection'' is succeeded on the HDS.
  1. Stop the engine.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and make sure that the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator goes off. If there is any aiming defect, the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator comes on and stores the DTC.
    NOTE: If the aiming fails or is aborted during of operation for any reason, reattempt the aiming procedure from the beginning.
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS, then turn the ignition switch OFF. The static camera aiming process is now completed. Go to the next step for the dynamic camera aiming procedure.
Precaution for the Dynamic Camera Aiming
Perform the dynamic camera aiming on a road that runs parallel and horizontal to adjacent lanes and that allows the vehicle to drive between 72−112 km/h (45−70 mph) for 10 minutes or more. The aiming fails to unless it is completed, within 30 minutes.
The dynamic camera aiming may not be completed in bad weather such as rain, fog, or snow. The white (yellow) lines on the road are not clearly visible because of the reflecting wet road surface.
When you perform the dynamic camera aiming in the dark, turn the light on. In this case, adjust the vehicle's optical axis properly and check that the vehicle's headlights and the windshield have not adhesion of dirt.
When the dynamic camera aiming has failed for some reason, an aiming failure code is displayed on the HDS. In this case, troubleshoot the indicated failure code with the HDS.
Implementation of the Dynamic Camera Aiming
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II) and make sure that only the DTC 11 is indicated with the HDS.
  1. Select ‘‘LKAS camera'' from the ‘‘DRIVING SUPPORT'' menu, then select ‘‘Dynamic Camera Aiming'' from ‘‘Miscellaneous Tests''.
  1. Drive the vehicle at between 72−112 km/h (45−70 mph).
  1. Perform the aiming according to the instruction of the HDS screen. When the dynamic camera aiming has failed for some reason, an aiming failure code is displayed on the HDS. In this case, troubleshoot the indicated failure code with the HDS and retry the aiming procedure first.
  1. Stop the vehicle and ignition switch OFF after confirming that the aiming completed, with CAMERA OFF and INDICATOR OFF, then turn the ignition switch ON (II) and make sure that the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator does not come on. If any DTCs are indicated, troubleshoot the indicated DTCs.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF and disconnect the HDS from the data link connector (DLC).
    NOTE:
    • While aiming, drive in the center of the lane and do not activate the wiper and turn signal.
    • The dynamic camera aiming status can be confirmed in the MID and adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator as follows.
