A/T Pressure Test
Special Tools Required

A/T oil pressure gauge set
 07406-0020004
  1. Make sure the transmission fluid is filled to the proper level.
  1. Raise the vehicle up on a lift, or apply the parking brake, block rear wheels, and raise the front of the vehicle. Make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Allow the front wheels to rotate freely.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Connect the HDS to the DLC.
  1. Connect the oil pressure gauges to the line pressure inspection port (A). Do not allow dust or other foreign particles to enter the ports while connecting the gauges.
  1. Warm up the engine (the radiator fan come on).
  1. Hold the engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) in the P or N position.
  1. Measure the line pressure at the line pressure inspection port (A) in the P or N position.
    NOTE: Higher pressure may be indicated if measurements are made in shift lever position other than P or N.

  1. Turn the engine off, then disconnect the oil pressure gauge from the line pressure inspection port.
  1. Install the sealing bolt in the line pressure inspection port with a new sealing washer, and tighten the bolt to 18 N·m (1.8 kgf·m, 13 lbf·ft). Do not reuse old sealing washer.
  1. Connect the oil pressure gauge to the 1st clutch pressure inspection port (B).
  1. Remove the air intake duct and air cleaner housing, and connect the oil pressure gauge to the 2nd clutch pressure inspection port (C). Then temporarily install the air cleaner housing and air intake duct.
  1. Start the engine, and shift into the M position.
  1. Shift to 1st gear, and measure the 1st clutch pressure at the 1st clutch pressure inspection port (B) while holding engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ).
  1. Shift up to 2nd gear, and measure the 2nd clutch pressure at the 2nd clutch pressure inspection port (C) while holding engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ).

  1. Turn the engine off, then disconnect the oil pressure gauges from the 1st clutch pressure and 2nd clutch pressure inspection ports.
  1. Install the sealing bolts in the 1st clutch pressure and 2nd clutch pressure inspection ports with new sealing washers, and tighten the bolts to 18 N·m (1.8 kgf·m, 13 lbf·ft). Do not reuse old sealing washers.
  1. Connect the oil pressure gauge to the 3rd clutch pressure inspection port (D) and 5th clutch pressure inspection port (F).
  1. Connect the oil pressure gauge to the 4th clutch pressure inspection port (E).
  1. Start the engine with the transmission in the P position while pressing the brake pedal.
  1. Shift to the M position, and release the brake pedal; the transmission is in 1st gear.
  1. Press the accelerator pedal to increase the engine speed to 2,500 rpm (min −1 ), then shift to 2nd gear.
  1. Release the accelerator pedal slowly to close the throttle over 5 seconds; the engine speed decreases to 1,000 rpm with the transmission in 2nd gear.
  1. Press the accelerator pedal very slowly to increase the engine speed to 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) over 5 seconds, and hold the accelerator. Shift to 3rd gear, and measure the 3rd clutch pressure at the 3rd clutch pressure inspection port (D) while holding engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ).
  1. Shift to 4th gear, and measure the 4th clutch pressure at the 4th clutch pressure inspection port (E) while holding engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ).
  1. Shift to 5th gear, measure 5th clutch pressure at the 5th clutch pressure inspection port (F) while holding engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ).

  1. Shift to the R position, then release the brake pedal. Raise the engine speed to 2,000 rpm (min −1 ), and measure 4th clutch pressure at the 4th clutch pressure inspection port (E).

  1. Turn the engine off, then disconnect the oil pressure gauges from the 3rd, 4th, and 5th clutch pressure inspection ports.
  1. Install the sealing bolts in the 3rd, 4th, and 5th clutch pressure inspection ports with new sealing washers, and tighten the bolts to 18 N·m (1.8 kgf·m, 13 lbf·ft). Do not reuse old sealing washers.
  1. If the measurements are out of the service limit, problems and probable causes are listed in the table.
    Problem
    Probable causes

    • O-rings

  1. Install the splash shield.
