Cam Chain Removal
NOTE: Keep the cam chain away from magnetic fields.
  1. Turn the crankshaft pulley so its top dead center (TDC) mark (A) lines up with the pointer (B).
  1. Remove the front tyres/wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the cylinder head cover.
  1. Check that the No.1 piston TDC marks (A) on the variable valve timing control (VTC) actuator and exhaust camshaft sprocket are aligned.
  1. Remove the oil cooler hose joint pipe from the water pump (K24A3 engine).
  1. Remove the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Disconnect the crankshaft position (CKP) sensor connector (A) and variable valve timing control (VTC) oil control solenoid valve connector (B).
  1. Remove the VTC oil control solenoid valve.
  1. Support the engine with a jack and wood block under the oil pan.
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), and remove the upper bracket (B).
  1. Remove the side engine mount bracket.
  1. Remove the chain case.
  1. Loosely install the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Turn the crankshaft counterclockwise to compress the auto-tensioner.
  1. Align the holes on the lock (A) and the auto-tensioner (B), then insert a 1.2 mm (0.05 in.) diameter pin or lock pin (P/N 14511-PNA-003) (C) into the holes. Turn the crankshaft clockwise to secure the pin.
  1. Remove the auto-tensioner.
  1. Remove the cam chain guide B.
  1. Remove the cam chain guide A and tensioner arm (B).
  1. Remove the cam chain.
