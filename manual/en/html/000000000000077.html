EPS Indicator Circuit Troubleshooting
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Start the engine, and watch the EPS indicator.
Does the EPS indicator come on?
YES -
If the EPS indicator comes on and goes off, it's OK. If the EPS indicator stays on or blinks, go to Step 11 .
NO -
Go to Step 3 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, then ON (II) again, and watch the brake system indicator.
Does the brake system indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Repair open in the indicator power source circuit:■
  • Blown No. 21 (7.5 A) fuse.
  • Open in the wire between the No. 21 (7.5 A) fuse and gauge control module.
  • Open circuit inside the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Connect gauge control module connector A (10P) terminal No. 10 to body ground with a jumper wire.
Does the EPS indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Go to gauge control module troubleshooting. ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect EPS control unit connector C (13P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Connect EPS control unit connector C (13P) terminal No. 11 and body ground with a jumper wire.
Does the EPS indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between gauge control module and EPS control unit.■
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and EPS control unit connector C (13P) terminals No. 1 and No. 5 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 10 .
NO -
Repair open in the wires between EPS control unit and body ground (G503).■
  1. Measure the voltage between EPS control unit connector C (13P) terminal No. 6 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Check for loose terminals and poor connectors at the EPS control unit and gauge control module connectors. If necessary, substitute a known-good EPS control unit and recheck.■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between No. 21 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and EPS control unit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect EPS control unit connector C (13P).
  1. Disconnect the connectors from the following units.
    • ECM/PCM connector E (31P)
    • ABS control unit
    • SRS control unit
  1. Check for continuity between EPS control unit connector C (13P) terminal No. 12 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the SCS circuit.■
NO -
Go to Step 15 .
  1. Connect EPS control unit connector C (13P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between data link connector (16P) terminal No. 9 and body ground.
Is there about 6 V?
YES -
Go to Step 18 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between data link connector (16P) and EPS control unit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Disconnect EPS control unit connector C (13P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and start the engine.
Does the EPS indicator go off?
YES -
Go to Step 22 .
NO -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between gauge control module and EPS control unit.■
  1. Troubleshoot the gauge control module.
Is it normal?
YES -
Check for loose terminals and poor connections at EPS control unit and gauge control module connectors. If necessary substitute a known-good EPS control unit and recheck.■
NO -
Replace the gauge control module .■
