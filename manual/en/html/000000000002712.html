Intake Manifold Removal and Installation

Exploded View
Removal
  1. Disconnect the intake air temperature (IAT) sensor connector (A).
  1. Remove the vacuum hose (B) and breather pipe (C), then remove the intake air duct (D).
  1. Remove the intake manifold cover.
  1. K20A6 engine: Remove the throttle cable (A) and cruise control cable (B) by loosening the locknuts (C), then slipping the cable ends out of the accelerator linkage. Take care not to bend the cables when removing them. Always replace any kinked cable with a new one.
  1. Remove the evaporative emission (EVAP) canister hose (A) and brake booster vacuum hose (B).
  1. Remove the engine wire harness connectors and wire harness clamps from the intake manifold.
    • Idle air control (IAC) valve connector
    • K20A6 engine: Throttle position sensor connector
    • K24A3 engine: Throttle actuator connector
    • Manifold absolute pressure (MAP) sensor connector
  1. Remove the harness holder (A), harness bracket (B), and remove the harness clamp (C).
  1. Remove and plug the water bypass hoses.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A) and the harness connector (B) from the intake manifold bracket, then remove the intake manifold bracket (C).
  1. Remove the vacuum hose.
  1. Remove the intake manifold.
Installation
  1. Install the intake manifold (A) with a new gasket (B), and tighten the bolts/nuts in a crisscross pattern in two or three steps, beginning with the inner bolt.
  1. Install the vacuum hose.
  1. Install the intake manifold bracket (A), then install the harness clamp (B), and harness connector (C) to the intake manifold bracket.
  1. Install the water bypass hoses.
  1. Install the harness holder (A), harness bracket (B), and install the harness clamp (C).
  1. Install the EVAP canister hose (A) and brake booster vacuum hose (B).
  1. K20A6 engine: Install the throttle cable, then adjust the cable.
  1. K20A6 engine: Install the cruise control cable, then adjust the cable.
  1. Install the intake manifold cover.
  1. Install the intake air duct (A), then connect the IAT sensor connector (B), and install the vacuum hose (C) and breather pipe (D).
  1. Clean up any spilled engine coolant.
  1. After installation, check that all tubes, hoses, and connectors are installed correctly.
  1. Refill the radiator with engine coolant, and bleed air from the cooling system with the heater valve open.
