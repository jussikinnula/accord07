A/C Condenser Fan High Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE:
  • Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if the radiator fan and/or the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  • Before performing symptom troubleshooting, check for powertrain DTCs.
  1. Check the No. 30 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck.■
  1. Remove the fan control relay from the multi-relay box, and test it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the fan control relay.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 5 terminal of the fan control relay 5P socket and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Go to Step 17 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 2 terminal of the fan control relay 5P socket and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the fan control relay and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G302.■
  1. Remove A/C diode B from the left side (LHD) or the right side (RHD) of the dashboard.
  1. Check for current flow in both directions between the No. 1 and No. 2 terminals of A/C diode B.
Is there current flow in only one direction?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Replace A/C diode B.■
  1. Make sure the engine coolant temperature is below 93 °C (199 °F).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 2 terminal of A/C diode B 2P connector and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C condenser fan relay and A/C diode B.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reinstall the A/C diode B.
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector B (16P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 12 terminal of climate control unit connector B (16P) and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Check for loose wires or poor connections at climate control unit B (16P) and at A/C diode B 2P connector. If the connections are good, substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between A/C diode B and the climate control unit.■
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 7 terminal of under-hood fuse/relay box connector E (16P) and body ground with the under-hood fuse/relay box connectors connected.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between the under-hood fuse/relay box and the fan control relay.■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 30 fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the under-hood fuse/relay box.■
