Master Cylinder Replacement
NOTE: Do not spill brake fluid on the vehicle; it may damage the paint; if brake fluid gets on the paint, wash it off immediately with water.
  1. Remove the access panel (A) on the driver's side.
  1. Remove the brake booster vacuum hose (B) from the clamp.
  1. For LHD model, remove the cable (C) from the body and strut bar (D).
  1. For RHD model, remove the throttle cable (E) and the A/C lines (F) from the clamp (G).
  1. Remove the strut bar on the driver's side.
  1. Remove the reservoir cap and brake fluid from the master cylinder reservoir with a syringe.
LHD model
RHD model
  1. Disconnect the brake fluid level switch connector (A).
  1. Disconnect the brake lines (B) form the master cylinder (C). To prevent spills, cover the hose joints with rags or shop towels.
  1. Remove the master cylinder mounting nuts (D).
  1. Remove the master cylinder from the brake booster (E). Be careful not to bend or damage the brake lines when removing the master cylinder.
  1. Remove the rod seal (F) from the master cylinder.
    NOTE: During installation, set the new rod seal onto the master cylinder with its grooved side (G) toward the master cylinder.
  1. Install the master cylinder in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Coat the inner bore lip and outer circumference of the new rod seal with the recommended seal grease in the master cylinder set.
    • Check the brake pedal height and free play after installing the master cylinder, and adjust it if necessary.
  1. Bleed the brake system.
