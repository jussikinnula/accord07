Audio System Symptom Troubleshooting - No sound
No sound
NOTE: Set the fader and balance positions to the center.
  1. Check to see if there is a specific speaker that has no sound.
Is there a specific one?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Check the speaker that has no sound for any damage.
Is there any damage?
YES -
Substitute the speaker and recheck.■
NO -
Go to Step 3 .
  1. Disconnect and check the speaker 2P connector for loose or a poor connection.
Reconnect the speaker 2P connector and recheck the symptom; does it still appear?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Operation is normal.■
  1. Remove the audio-HVAC display module.
  1. Check for continuity between the speaker terminal and audio unit terminals.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
An open circuit or poor wire connection between the speaker and audio unit.■
  1. Disconnect the audio unit connector A (20P).
  1. Reconnect the speakers and measure the speaker resistance at the following terminals in the audio unit connector A (20P):
    Driver's: No. 8 (+) and No. 18 (−) terminals
    Front passenger's: No. 7 (+) and No. 17 (−) terminals
    Left rear: No. 6 (+) and No. 16 (−) terminals
    Right rear: No. 5 (+) and No. 15 (−) terminals
Is there about 4 Ω?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
An open circuit or poor wire connection between the speakers and audio unit.■
  1. Check for continuity of the terminals below on the audio unit connector A (20P).
    Driver's: No. 20 (ground) and No. 8 (+), No. 20 (ground) and No. 18 (−) terminals
    Front passenger's: No. 20 (ground) and No. 7 (+), No. 20 (ground) and No. 17 (−) terminals
    Left rear: No. 20 (ground) and No. 6 (+). No. 20 (ground) and No. 16 (−) terminals
    Right rear: No. 20 (ground) and No. 5 (+). No. 20 (ground) and No. 15 (−) terminals
Is there continuity?
YES -
Faulty wire harness between the speaker and the audio unit.■
NO -
Faulty audio unit.■
