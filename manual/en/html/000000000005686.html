A/T Mainshaft 5th Gear Clearance Inspection
K24A3 Engine Model
  1. Remove the mainshaft transmission housing bearing.
  1. Install the thrust needle bearing (A), 5th gear (B), needle bearing (C), thrust needle bearing (D), 41 x 68 mm thrust washer (E), 4th/5th clutch (F), 4th gear collar (G), and transmission housing bearing (H) on the mainshaft (I). Do not install the O-rings during inspection.
  1. Install the idler gear (J) on the mainshaft by a press, then install the conical spring washer (K) and locknut (L).
  1. Tighten the locknut to 29 N·m (3.0 kgf·m, 22 lbf·ft).
  1. Set the dial indicator (A) on the 5th gear (B).
  1. Life the 5th gear (A) up while holding the mainshaft, and use the dial indicator (B) to read the 5th gear axial clearance.
  1. Measure the 5th gear axial clearance in at least three places while moving the 5th gear. Use the average as the actual clearance.
    Standard:
    0.04−0.10 mm (0.002−0.004 in.)

  1. If the clearance is out of standard, remove the 41 x 68 mm thrust washer and measure its thickness (A).
  1. Select and install a new thrust washer, then recheck.

  1. After replacing the thrust washer, make sure the clearance is within the standard.
  1. Disassemble the installed parts from the mainshaft.
  1. Reinstall the bearing in the transmission housing.
