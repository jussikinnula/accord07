VSA Modulator-control Unit Removal and Installation
NOTE:
  • Do not spill brake fluid on the vehicle; it may damage the paint; if brake fluid gets on the paint, wash it off immediately with water.
  • Be careful not to damage or deform the brake lines during removal and installation.
  • To prevent the brake fluid from flowing, plug and cover the hose ends and joints with a shop towel or equivalent material.
Removal
  1. Remove the ground cable (A).
  1. Remove the torque rod (B).
  1. Remove the A/C line bracket (A).
  1. Remove the washer reservoir nozzle mounting screw (B).
  1. Remove the P/S pump inlet hose (A) and P/S retum hose (B) from the clips.
  1. Move the P/S fluid reservoir (C) and the washer reservoir nozzle (D) toward the engine side.
    NOTE: Do not disconnect the P/S pump inlet hose and P/S retum hose from P/S fluid reservoir.
  1. Remove the washer reservoir nozzle mounting bracket (A).
  1. Remove the P/S hose mounting bracket (A).
  1. Remove the six brake lines from clip (A).
  1. Loosen the flare nuts, and disconnect the six brake lines from the VSA modulator-control unit (A).
  1. Pull up the lock (A) of the VSA control unit 47P connector (B), and the connector disconnects itself.
  1. Remove the VSA modulator-control unit bracket mounting bolts (A).
  1. Remove the VSA modulator-control unit (A) with the bracket.
  1. Remove the bracket (A) from the VSA modulator-control unit (B).
Installation
  1. Install the VSA modulator-control unit in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Tighten all mounting hardware to the specified torque values.
    • Align the connecting surface of the VSA control unit 47P connector.
    • Carefully push in the lock of the VSA control unit 47P connector until you hear it click into place, then confirm the connector is fully seated.
    • Reconnect the six brake lines, then tighten the flare nuts to specified torque values.
    • Bleed the brake system, starting with the front wheels.
  1. Start the engine, and check that the ABS and VSA indicators go off.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle, and check that the ABS and VSA indicators do not come on.
  1. If the brake pedal is now spongy, there may be air trapped in the modulator and then induced into the normal brake system during modulation. Bleed the brake system again, starting with the front wheels.
